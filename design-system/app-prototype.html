Debrief
Epidural haematoma with early herniation
A temporal impact tore the middle meningeal artery, and an expanding epidural collection drove the ICP up. The fixed pupil, bradycardia, hypertension and irregular breathing were the herniation warning — recognised and treated before arrest.
KEY TEACHING
The blown pupil & Cushing's triad
A unilaterally fixed, dilated pupil with a falling GCS is uncal herniation until proven otherwise. Bradycardia + widening pulse pressure + irregular breathing complete Cushing's triad.
⚡ TL;DR
Treat the pressure in the head, not the heart rate — hypertonic saline, HOB 30°, protect the airway, get the scan and the surgeons.
Why anisocoria localises
CN III's parasympathetic fibres ride on its surface, so they're compressed first — the pupil blows on the side of the lesion.
